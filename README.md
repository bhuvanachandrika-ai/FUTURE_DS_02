\# Customer Retention \& Churn Analysis
\## Project Overview
This project analyzes customer retention and churn behavior using Power BI. The objective is to identify factors that influence customer churn and provide actionable business recommendations to improve customer retention.
\## Tools Used
\* Power BI Desktop

\* Microsoft Excel / CSV Dataset

\* Data Cleaning and Transformation

\* Data Visualization



\## Dataset



The dataset contains customer information such as:



\* Customer ID

\* Gender

\* Senior Citizen Status

\* Internet Service

\* Contract Type

\* Payment Method

\* Tenure

\* Churn Status



\## Dashboard Features



\### Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)



\* Total Customers: 90

\## Business Insights



\### 1. Strong Customer Retention



\* Most customers remain active with the company.

\* Retention rate is exceptionally high at 98.89%.

\* Existing customer service and engagement strategies appear effective.



\### 2. Low Churn Rate



\* Only a small percentage of customers have churned.

\* Current churn levels indicate stable customer relationships.



\### 3. Contract Type Impact



\* Customers with longer-term contracts show better retention.

\* Month-to-month customers require closer monitoring.



\### 4. Payment Method Analysis



\* Certain payment methods may have a stronger association with churn.

\* Automated payment methods can improve customer retention.



\### 5. Service Usage Patterns



\* Internet service preferences influence customer behavior.

\* Understanding service adoption can help improve customer satisfaction.



\## Recommendations



\### Improve Customer Engagement



\* Conduct periodic customer satisfaction surveys.

\* Address customer concerns proactively.



\### Promote Long-Term Contracts



\* Offer discounts and incentives for annual contracts.

\* Encourage customers to move from monthly plans to long-term subscriptions.



\### Enhance Loyalty Programs



\* Reward long-term customers with exclusive benefits.

\* Introduce retention campaigns for high-risk customers.



\### Monitor Churn Indicators



\* Track customer tenure and service usage patterns.

\* Develop early warning systems to identify customers likely to churn.



\## Conclusion



The analysis reveals excellent customer retention performance with minimal churn. Continued focus on customer satisfaction, loyalty programs, and long-term contract adoption can further strengthen retention and reduce future churn risks.
